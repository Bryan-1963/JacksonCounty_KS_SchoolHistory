6/20/1859:

Permission for the first school district in Jackson County was granted. The first building was a log cabin that was a church and school prior to an organized district. At the time the cabin was located on the Pottawatomi Reservation (before the reservation was diminished in 1861? It appears that the reservation extended east to what is now Q4 Road prior to 1861 (Ref 63, F.64)? Which would be 1 mile west of the later location of Brick schoolhouse…?) and it was about ½ mile from the later location of Brick School. One log was left out of the wall to provide lighting. (Ref 45).


Figure 1 –Sketch of the Original District #1 Schoolhouse in 1859 (Ref 45, 85)

5/12/1868:

Godfrey and Mary Hofer donated one acre of land and the first brick school building was constructed in 1868. The school was also used for church services until a church was built in 1890 (Ref 45).


Figure 2 –The Original District #1 Brick Schoolhouse Built 1868 (Ref 45, 85)

Photo shows W. H. Webster, who was one of the original teachers at Brick School, with his wife. He taught seven terms.

1878:

The map showed a school in Franklin Township about ¼ mile north of what is now 206 Road on what would be R4 Road (Ref 78) [39°25'10.8"N 95°42'37.4"W]. Due to the scale of the 1878 map this is likely no different than the later map locations.


Figure 3 – 1878 Map with Brick School #1 (Ref 78)

8/6/1962:

A tornado destroyed the school and the grange that was southwest of the school across the road intersection (Ref 45).


Figure 13 –Brick Schoolhouse after 1962 Tornado (Ref 45)

1963:

The Jackson County Farm Directory for 1963 still showed the schoolhouse on the northeast corner of what is now the intersection of R4 Road and 206 Road even though the school was destroyed the previous year (Ref 83) [39°25'10.8"N 95°42'37.4"W].
