1881:

One Holton school was shown on the map (Ref 14) taking up three city lots on the southwest corner of 5th and New Jersey. That location was later part of Central Elementary School


Figure 2 – 1881 Holton Public School Location (Ref 14)

1885:

Fire Insurance maps for this year showed the public school (Central) on the southwest corner of Fifth and New Jersey Streets and showed some details of the building layout. The building was two stories tall, constructed of brick with a shingle roof, a furnace in the basement and a single wood framed outhouse (Ref 84).


Figure 3 – 1885 Holton Public School Location and Building Details (Ref 84)

1888:

The Colorado schoolhouse was built at the corner of 5th and Colorado and faced south (Ref 85).


Figure 4 –Holton Colorado School Soon After Its Construction (Ref 85)

1889:

Fire Insurance maps for this year showed:

  1. Colorado School on the northeast corner of Colorado and 5th Streets. The Colorado school building was two stories of brick with a third wood frame construction story on the stairwell, had a composite roof, was heated by steam and had two wood frame outhouses (Ref 84).
  2. Central School on the southwest corner of Fifth and New Jersey Streets. The Central school building was two stories tall, constructed of brick with a shingle roof, a hot air furnace and a single building outhouse (Ref 84).

Figure 5 – 1889 Holton Public School Locations and Building Details (Ref 84)

1896:

Fire Insurance maps for this year showed:

  1. Colorado School on the northeast corner of Colorado and 5th Streets. The Central school building was two stories tall, constructed of brick with a shingle roof, a hot air furnace and now had three wood framed outhouses (Ref 84)
  2. Central School on the southwest corner of Fifth and New Jersey Streets. The Colorado school building was two stories of brick with a third wood frame construction story on the stairwell, had a composite roof and was heated by steam. No outhouses were shown for the Colorado school (Ref 84).

Figure 6 – 1896 Holton Public School Locations and Building Details (Ref 84)

1903:

Two Holton schools are shown on the map (Ref 12):

  1. Colorado School taking up the west half of a city block bounded by Colorado Street, 5th Street and 6th Street. The property is still a part of what is now the Holton Elemenary School grounds but the original building is no longer there.
  2. Central School taking up three city lots on the southwest corner of 5th and New Jersey.

1905:

Fire Insurance maps for this year showed the Colorado School on the northeast corner of Colorado and 5th Street, Central School on the southwest corner of Fifth and New Jersey Streets and the High School on the northeast corner of 4th and Kansas (Ref 84).

  1. The Central school building was two stories tall, constructed of brick with a shingle roof, a hot air furnace, no lights and had no outhouses shown.
  2. The Colorado school building was two stories of brick with a third wood frame construction story on the stairwell, had a composite roof, had electric lights and was heated by steam. No outhouses were shown for the Colorado school.
  3. The High School. (See Holton High School details section)

1912:

Fire Insurance maps for this year showed the Colorado School on the northeast corner of Colorado and 5th Street, Central School on the southwest corner of Fifth and New Jersey Streets and the High School on the northeast corner of 4th and Kansas (Ref 84).

  1. The Central school building was two stories tall, constructed of brick with a shingle roof, a hot air furnace, no lights and had three wood framed outhouses.
  2. The Colorado school building was two stories of brick with a third wood frame construction story on the stairwell, had a composite roof, had electric lights and was heated by steam. No outhouses were shown for the Colorado school.
  3. The High School (see Holton High School section for more details).

1921:

The 1921 map of Holton shows four public school locations (Ref 10):

  1. Central Grade School taking up three city lots on the southeast corner of 5th and New Jersey [39°27'52.7"N 95°43'55.2"W].
  2. Colorado Grade School taking up half a city block on the east side of Colorado Street between 5th and 6th Streets [39°27'54.3"N 95°44'45.5"W].
  3. Junior High School taking up two city lots on the northeast corner of 4th and Kansas Streets [39°27'48.7"N 95°44'24.5"W]
  4. Senior High School (see Holton High School section for more details).

Figure 18 – 1921 Holton Public School Locations and Building Details (Ref 10)

1922:

Fire Insurance maps for this year showed the Colorado School on the northeast corner of Colorado and 5th Street, Central School on the southwest corner of Fifth and New Jersey Streets, the Junior High School on the northeast corner of 4th and Kansas and the High School at the former Campbell College site (Ref 84).

  1. The Central school building was two stories tall, constructed of brick with a shingle roof, a hot air furnace, no lights and had three wood framed outhouses.
  2. The Colorado school building was two stories of brick with a third wood frame construction story on the stairwell, had a composite roof, had electric lights and was heated by steam. No outhouses were shown for the Colorado school.
  3. The Junior High School was two stories tall, constructed of brick, was heated by steam, had no lights, had a slate or tin roof and no outhouses shown.
  4. The High School (see Holton High School section for more details).
