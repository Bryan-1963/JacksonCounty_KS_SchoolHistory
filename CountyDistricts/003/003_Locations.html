1858:

The first school (not a public school) was established at the site that is now the North Cedar Cemetery (South Denison Cemetry) (Ref 70) [39°22'59.6"N 95°37'19.4"W].


1878:

The map shows a school in northeastern Cedar Township on the north side of what is now 190 Road about ¼ mile west of W Road (Ref 78) [39°23'25.3"N 95°37'52.4"W].


Figure 1 – 1878 Map with Denison School #3 (Ref 78)


1881:

The Denison school was in northern Cedar Township just east of the city of Tippinville. It was located on the south side of what is now 198 Road a little over 1/8th mile east of W Road (Ref 14) [39°24'16.4"N 95°37'27.0"W].


Figure 2 – 1881 Map with Denison School #3 (Ref 14)


Note that the town of Tippinville was located one mile north of where Denison is now located. Denison was laid out in 1887 to be adjacent to the Kansas City, Wyandotte and Northwestern Railway that was to connect Valley Falls with Holton (Ref 70).


1883:

A school was shown on the map on what is now 198 Road just under ½ mile east of W Road (Ref 81). The scale and accuracy of this map does not merit this being different from the 1881 map location [39°24'16.4"N 95°37'27.0"W]


Figure 3 – 1883 Map with Denison School #3 (Ref 81)


1887:

The Denison school was in northern Cedar Township just east of the city of Tippinville. It was located on the south side of what is now 198 Road a little over 1/8th mile east of W Road (Ref 15) [39°24'16.4"N 95°37'27.0"W].


Figure 4 – 1887 Map with Denison School #3 (Ref 15)


1903:

The Denison school was in Garfield Township within the city of "Dennison." It was located on the west side of Central Avenue in the middle of the block between 5th and 6th Streets (Ref 12) [39°23'44.6"N 95°37'40.5"W].


Figure 5 – 1903 Map with Denison School #3 (Ref 12)


Oct 8, 1908:

The Holton Recorder reported that the new Denison schoolhouse with four rooms was completed and classes have started.

Mar 1, 1912:

The school district map of 1912 shows Denison Schoolhouse on the northeast corner of the intersection of what is now 198 Road and W Road [39°24'17.6"N 95°37'34.8"W]. This is likely a mistake since the school was by then located within the city of Denison. The school district boundaries are shown below (Ref 82).


Figure 8 –1912 School District Boundaries for Denison #3 (Ref 82)


1921:

The map showed a High School and a Grade School as separate buildings (Ref 10). The High School was in the middle of the block bounded by Central & Highland Avenues and 5th & 6th Streets [39°23'44.4"N 95°37'42.2"W]. The Grade School was located on the northeast corner of the intersection of 5th Street and Highland Avenue [39°23'42.5"N 95°37'43.9"W].


Figure 9 – 1921 Map with Denison Schools, District #3 (Ref 10)


1967:

Denison Elementary School built at 507 Central Avenue (Ref ???)

1990:

Denison Elementary School was closed and students transferred to Holton schools (Ref 70).
