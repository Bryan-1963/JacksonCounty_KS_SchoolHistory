1812-1821:

5/30/1854:

The Kansas-Nebraska Act became law and Kansas Territory was established (Ref 19, F.20).

Figure 2 – Kansas and Nebraska Territories Map (from Ref 21)

1854:

Jackson County did not yet exist. The land that was to become Jackson County was part of Calhoun County (Ref 23).

1857:

Kansas Counties were reconfigured, reducing Calhoun County’s size and creating Pottawatomie County

Figure 5 – 1857 Counties of Eastern Kansas Territory (Ref 23)

1859:

A reorganization of Kansas counties led to the elimination of Calhoun County and the creation of Jackson County. Jackson County was named for then-president Andrew Jackson (Ref 22).

Figure 6 – 1860 Counties of Eastern Kansas Territory (Ref 23)
