Notes for 1878 Map of Jackson County (Ref 78):

  1. Banner, Garfield and Lincoln Townships did not yet exist.
  2. The size and scale of this map only supports determining which quarter-section a school was located in.
  3. The schoolhouse locations are indicated but they are not numbered on the original map. District numbers in this image are best guesses based on later locations.
  4. There are several schools indicated on this map that do not correspond to known schools and could have been private schools:
    1. Whiting Township: southeast of the intersection of what is now 199th Street (Brown County) and X Road. There is also no known school nearby in Brown County.
    2. Northwestern Washington Township: halfway between what is now C and D Roads and about ¼ mile south of 190 Road (Ref 78) [39°23'12.5"N 95°59'15.2"W]. This was likely a private school since there were no schools in the immediate vicinity that were chartered prior to 1878.
    3. Pottawatomie Reservation: northwest corner of what is now the intersection of 142 Road and O Road.
